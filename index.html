What the reviews say

The internet's verdict: a lively-but-relaxed beachfront base. Reviewers rave about the spotless pool, the design-y look, daily sunset DJ sessions, a genuinely good spa (free for guests), and warm, attentive staff. Rooms get consistent praise for being big, modern and clean.

The honest catch: Playa d'en Bossa is the party strip, so the immediate area is busy and very British-tourist-heavy, and sunbeds vanish under towels early. The hotel itself stays calm enough to sleep — several guests specifically called it a “haven” from the madness outside.

spotless pool sunset DJ free spa big clean rooms walk to clubs lovely staff busy strip
My take for us

This is the grown-up-girls'-trip sweet spot. You're a 12-min stroll from Ushuaïa when we want a big night, but the hotel is calm enough to actually recover the next day — spa, poolside cocktail service, sunset DJ by the water. Reviewers literally describe groups of friends here celebrating big birthdays and rebooking every year. Steps from the beach, quick cab to the Old Town for dinners.

✦ Strong fit — if we want nightlife on tap + a chill home base

What the reviews say

Reviewers consistently call it stylish, modern and great value — spacious refurbished apartments, comfy beds, a good pool with chill-out music, friendly switched-on staff, and the unbeatable perk of a full kitchen so you can make your own drinks and breakfast. Several describe it as the best-value serviced apartments on the strip.

The honest catch: it's lively and can be noisy — music by the pool from late morning, plus people coming and going at all hours (a few say "bring earplugs"). It's adults-only and unapologetically a party base. One or two mentioned petty theft, so use the room safe.

full kitchen great value stylish & modern closest to clubs adults-only lively / noisy use the safe
My take for us

This is the party-forward, self-catered option — adults-only and the closest to the clubs (Ushuaïa/Hï a 5-min walk). The apartments with full kitchens are a real plus for a group: pre-drinks on the terrace, coffee when we want it, and we save the breakfast money. Cheaper per person than Garbi too. Trade-off: it leans louder and more "up-for-it," with less of a spa-retreat calm — so it's the pick if nightlife is the whole point and we want our own space, less so if anyone wants quiet lie-ins.

✦ Strong fit — if we're here to party + want apartments & savings

What the reviews say

The coolest little design hotel on the strip — a Miami 1980s art-deco boutique with funky, playful rooms (SMEG fridges, in-room cocktail bar, kitchenette), gorgeous lobby and pool, and warm staff who make killer cocktails. The clever trick: it's tucked on a quiet street so you don't hear the ravers, but you can use the sister hotels two minutes away for beach loungers and afternoon DJs.

The honest catch: it's small and intimate rather than a big resort, ground-floor rooms have weaker soundproofing, and the on-site pool food/service drew the odd gripe. Best for a stylish, lower-key base — not a big-pool party scene.

design boutiquein-room cocktail barquiet streetsister-hotel poolsgreat cocktailssmall & intimate
My take for us

The aesthetic, grown-up pick. If we want the cutest rooms, an Instagram-worthy lobby, and a calm night's sleep while still being a short walk from the action, this is it. Suites have a living area + bar so we can pre-game in style, and we'd only need 3 rooms. The compromise: no big resort pool scene of its own (you borrow the sister hotels' beach setup), so it's less of a "live at the pool all day with 8 of us" vibe and more boutique-cool.

✦ Strong fit — if we prize style + calm over a big pool scene

What the reviews say

The classic big beachfront resort — direct beach access via a key-card gate, a huge pool, daily entertainment/animation team, spa, and a lively all-rounder atmosphere. Reviewers love the location (steps from sand, minutes from clubs and airport), the friendly bar staff running drinks games, and good breakfasts. Best value-for-money of the bunch on the headline price.

The honest catch: it's the most "package holiday" / family-resort feel, and reviews on rooms are genuinely mixed — some report maintenance issues, occasional cleanliness lapses, and flight-path noise. A few felt it wasn't quite 4-star. Go in expecting cheerful-and-busy, not boutique-polished.

on the beachhuge poolentertainment teambest valuefamily-ishmixed on roomssome plane noise
My take for us

The budget-friendly beachfront option, and the sea-view rooms are lovely (proper balcony, view straight to the water). If our priority is being literally on the sand for the lowest headline price, this wins — and a few of us could splurge on the jacuzzi Superior. But it's the most resort-y/family vibe of the five and the least consistent on room quality, so it's the pick if location + price beat polish for us. Just remember the +VAT & fees bump the real total.

✦ Good fit — if beachfront + budget matter most

What the reviews say

A genuine design oasis — guests gush about the cool contemporary look, the stunning beachfront pool with sea views and DJs, direct beach access, and standout staff (Raquel at the desk gets named repeatedly for scoring dinner reservations and even VIP club tickets). Solo female travelers specifically said they felt safe. Rooms are stylish suites with kitchenettes and surprising quiet given the location.

The honest catch: it's expensive, and food/drinks on-site are pricey for what you get. A couple of guests found it didn't feel quite "$400-a-night luxurious," and being right by the airport means some flight noise. It's a boutique aparthotel (38 suites), not a big resort.

My take for us

The splurge / treat-yourself pick. If we want the most beautiful, design-led, adults-only stay with a proper beach-club-and-DJ scene right outside our suite — and a concierge who can actually get us into clubs — AMA is the showstopper. It's the priciest by a clear margin, so it's the "is this the trip we go all-out?" option. Adults-only + soundproof suites + kitchenettes make it a dreamy girls' base; just know we're paying for the design and the address.
